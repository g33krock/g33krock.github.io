Cover Letter


Dear Eric Lund and/or Financial Transformation Team,

I can't imagine a more enjoyable field to work in than to work as a software engineer! I worked in the field of education for over 10 years. First I was a teacher, then an administrator, and finally a technical director. Over my entire career in education I found myself gravitating heavily toward technology. At first it was simple things like using Excel and Forms to make grading automatic and easy, then I found myself really getting into wanting to teach technology to students and started a robotics club. As the years went by I came to realize that I needed to follow my passion and change careers. I took and finished a coding bootcamp in 2020 while I was running a campus for students with special needs, and then COVID struck. The leader of organization informed us that we were probably going to go under because we lacked the infrastructure to operate. I took this as an invitation/challenge to really see what I could do. Over the course of Spring Break, I put together a tracking/scheduling system for the schools using Google Sites, Google Forms, Google Sheets, Google Meet, and Microsoft Power Automate try and keep the organization going. It worked. After creating that, I poured nearly all of my time into building a legitimate Student Information System, specifically tailored to our unique organization using the tools I had learned in my coding bootcamp. Three months later, I rolled CyberCampus out to the organization and they've used it ever since. Since creating CyberCampus, I became the technical director for the organization.

Once CyberCampus was stable and no longer needing regular maintenance, I decided that it was time for me to look for a full time position as a developer/software engineer. There was a brief period where I took some contract work for a retirement company before I became a software engineer at Health Catalyst. I absolutely love working for this amazing company. The culture here is great, my team is phenomenal, and it's really everything that I could want. There is so much support and positivity, and I don't believe I've witnessed any kind of negative blame shifting. It's the kind of place where you are excited to work, and still feel good at the end of the day. My current position is as a backend engineer. Although I really enjoy working in backend development, my real passion lies in front end work (specifically in Angular and/or React environments).

I heard that Financial Transformation was looking for another Front-End/Full Stack Engineer and would love to be considered for that role. In addition to working on the client facing side of things, working on a team that is actively looking for and providing solutions to clients on their complex and sometimes fluid obstacles seems like a perfect fit for me.

Thank you,
Dallas Lovell
