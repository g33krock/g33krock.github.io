Cover Letter


Dear Church IT Department,

I can't imagine a more enjoyable field to work in than to work as a software engineer! I worked in the field of education for over 10 years. First I was a teacher, then an administrator, and finally a technical director. Over my entire career in education I found myself gravitating heavily toward technology. At first it was simple things like using Excel and Forms to make grading automatic and easy, then I found myself really getting into wanting to teach technology to students and started a robotics club. As the years went by I came to realize that I needed to follow my passion and change careers. I took and finished a coding bootcamp in 2020 while I was running a campus for students with special needs, and then COVID struck. The leader of orgamization informed us that we were probably going to go under because we lacked the infrastructure to operate. I took this as an invitation/challenge to really see what I could do. Over the course of Spring Break, I put together a tracking/scheduling system for the schools using Google Sites, Google Forms, Google Sheets, Google Meet, and Microsoft Power Automate try and keep the organization going. It worked. After creating that, I poured nearly all of my time into building a legitimate Student Information System, specifically tailored to our unique organization using the tools I had learned in my coding bootcamp. Three months later, I rolled CyberCampus out to the organization and they've used it ever since. Since creating CyberCampus, I became the technical director for the organization.

Once CyberCampus was stable and no longer needing regular maintenance, I decided that it was time for me to look for a full time position as a developer/software engineer. There was a brief period where I took some contract work for a retirement company before I found the company that I currently work for (remotely). I absolutely love working for this company. The culture here is great, my team is phenomenal, and it's really everything that I could want. There is so much support and positivity, and I don't believe I've witnessed any kind of negative blame shifting. It's the kind of place where you are excited to work, and still feel good at the end of the day. Unfortunately, with our current economic situation and the struggles in the healthcare industry, it may be necessary for my current company to downsize. As a relatively new employee I feel that I may be at risk of being laid off, which is why I'm looking for another position.

I was absolutely elated to hear that the church IT department was looking. My younger brother worked for church IT in the past, and the way he describes the feeling and culture there reminds me of my current company. That is very appealing and what I'm looking for. I want to work somewhere where I can work hard and innovate toward positivity in the world.

Thank you,
Dallas Lovell
